Fit Notes & Recommendations:

    How to Measure Yourself Accurately

    For the best results, use a soft measuring tape and have a friend assist you.

    Height

    Stand straight without shoes and measure from the floor to the top of your head.

    Chest

    Wrap the tape around the fullest part of your chest, under your armpits. Keep it level.

    Shoulders

    Measure from the edge of one shoulder to the other across your back, following the curve.

    Waist

    Measure your natural waistline, just above the belly button. Don't suck in your stomach!

    Inseam

    On well-fitting pants, measure from the crotch seam down to the bottom of the hem.

    Pro Tip

    Wear a thin shirt for jacket measurements. Empty your pockets before measuring your waist.

    The Perfect Fit: A Visual Guide

    Knowing your size is the first step. Here’s what to look for in a well-fitting suit.

    1. The Shoulders

    The jacket seam should end precisely where your natural shoulder does. No divots or overhang.

    2. The Closure

    When buttoned, the jacket should not pull into a tight 'X'. Lapels should lie flat and smooth.

    3. Sleeve Length

    Sleeves should end just above the wrist, allowing ¼ to ½ inch of your shirt cuff to show.

    4. Trouser Break

    For a modern look, trousers should have a slight break, gently resting on the top of your shoes.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What's the difference between suit cuts?

    Classic Fit: Most traditional, with more room for comfort. Modern Fit: A popular middle ground, slightly tapered for a defined silhouette. Slim Fit: The most form-fitting, cut close to the body for a sharp look.

    How do I convert between US and EU suit sizes?

    It's easy! For jackets, add 10 to your US size to get your EU size (e.g., US 40R = EU 50R). For trousers, it can vary, but our calculator handles the conversion automatically for you.

    Should I size up or down if I'm between sizes?

    It depends on your fit preference. For a tighter, 'slim' fit, consider sizing down. For a more comfortable, 'classic' fit, sizing up is often better. It's almost always easier for a tailor to take a suit in than to let it out.

    What's the most important measurement for a suit jacket?

    The shoulder measurement is the most critical. A tailor can adjust almost anything on a jacket—sleeve length, waist suppression—but altering the shoulders is complex and expensive. Make sure the shoulder seams sit squarely on your natural shoulder line.
