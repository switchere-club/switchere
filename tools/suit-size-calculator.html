MEASURING GUIDE

How to Measure for a Suit

Follow these professional measuring techniques for accurate results

Chest Measurement

Measure around the fullest part of your chest, under your arms, keeping the measuring tape parallel to the floor.

Waist Measurement

Measure around your natural waistline, typically where you wear your trousers, about 1-2 inches above the navel.

Inseam Length

Measure from the crotch seam down to the bottom of your ankle using a well-fitting pair of trousers as reference.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about suit sizing

What is my suit size if I know my chest measurement?
Your jacket size is typically your chest measurement rounded to the nearest even number. For example, if your chest measures 40 inches, you're a size 40. Suit jackets also include length codes: S (Short) for men under 5'7", R (Regular) for 5'7" to 6'1", and L (Long) for over 6'1". This ensures proper proportions for your height and torso length.
How do I convert US to EU suit sizes?
Converting US to EU suit sizes is simple: just add 10 to the US size. For example, US size 40 equals EU size 50, and US size 42 equals EU size 52. The length designations (S, R, L) remain the same across all regions. Our calculator automatically provides both sizing systems for your convenience.
Should I size up or down if I'm between sizes?
If you're between sizes, it's generally better to size up and have the suit professionally tailored. A jacket that's slightly large can be taken in by a tailor, but one that's too small usually can't be let out enough to achieve a proper fit. Professional alterations typically cost $50-150 and are well worth the investment for a perfect fit.
What does "drop" mean in suit sizing?
The "drop" refers to the difference between your jacket size and trouser waist size. A standard drop is 6 inches (for example, a size 40 jacket paired with 34-inch waist trousers). Athletic builds typically have an 8-inch or greater drop due to broader shoulders and narrower waists, while fuller builds might have a 4-inch drop. Understanding your drop helps ensure both pieces of your suit fit properly.
How accurate is this suit size calculator?
Our suit size calculator uses professional tailoring standards and industry-standard sizing conventions, making it highly accurate for determining your starting suit size. However, perfect fit also depends on brand-specific sizing, fabric type, and personal preference. We recommend using our calculator as a reliable starting point, then checking brand-specific size charts and trying on when possible. Professional alterations are recommended for achieving the perfect custom fit.
Do suit sizes vary between brands?
Yes, suit sizes can vary between brands, especially between European, American, and Asian manufacturers. Italian suits often run smaller and are cut slimmer, while American brands tend to have more generous sizing. Designer brands may also have unique fit characteristics or vanity sizing. We recommend always checking the specific brand's size chart when available and trying on before purchasing when possible.

Understanding International Suit Size Standards

Suit sizing follows established international conventions that have been refined over decades of professional tailoring. US and UK suit sizes use the same numerical system (34, 36, 38, 40, 42, etc.), which directly represents your chest measurement in inches. European (EU) sizes simply add 10 to this number (44, 46, 48, 50, 52, etc.).

The letter designation (S for Short, R for Regular, L for Long, and XL for Extra Long) indicates the jacket length and corresponds to your height, ensuring the jacket proportions match your torso length. This two-part sizing system allows for a more precise fit across different body types and heights.

Understanding these standardized sizing conventions helps you shop confidently across different retailers, countries, and regions worldwide, whether you're buying online or in-store.
